The rich volcanic soil and unique microclimate of the Papagayo peninsula give rise to an abundance of unique ingredients.

Local chefs are increasingly looking to ancestral techniques, such as cooking in traditional clay pots over open wood fires, to infuse their dishes with an authentic sense of place. This movement isn't just about reviving old recipes; it's about reinterpreting them through the lens of contemporary haute cuisine.

From foraging for native herbs in the dry tropical forest to partnering with artisanal fishermen in the surrounding bays, the culinary journey in Guanacaste is deeply rooted in its environment.
