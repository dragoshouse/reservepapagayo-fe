The Papagayo Peninsula is home to one of the rarest ecosystems in the Americas: the tropical dry forest. Unlike the lush rainforests typically associated with Costa Rica, the dry forest undergoes dramatic seasonal changes, presenting a completely different, yet equally vital, ecological tapestry.

During the dry season, many trees shed their leaves to conserve water, creating an austere landscape that dramatically bursts into a verdant oasis with the arrival of the first rains.

Conservation efforts here are multifaceted, focusing not just on protecting the flora and fauna, but also on sustainable development practices that allow humanity and nature to coexist harmoniously.
